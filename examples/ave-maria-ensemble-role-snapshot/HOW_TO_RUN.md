# How To Run

This public example is a compact snapshot, not a full reproduction of the
entire `Ave Maria` pipeline.

## What you can inspect directly

1. Listen to the short clip:
   - [audio/ave_maria_fragment_80s_90s.wav](audio/ave_maria_fragment_80s_90s.wav)
2. Open the MIDI reference subset:
   - [reference/ave_maria_fragment_midi_events.csv](reference/ave_maria_fragment_midi_events.csv)
3. Open the layered assignment subset:
   - [pipeline/ave_maria_fragment_layered_assignment.csv](pipeline/ave_maria_fragment_layered_assignment.csv)
4. Open the role-behavior subset:
   - [pipeline/ave_maria_fragment_role_behavior_map.csv](pipeline/ave_maria_fragment_role_behavior_map.csv)
5. Read the compact summary:
   - [pipeline/ave_maria_fragment_summary.txt](pipeline/ave_maria_fragment_summary.txt)

## What this example demonstrates

- ensemble events should not always be forced into one winning instrument
- instrument behavior is often better described as roles
- real mixed sound contains internal waves, body returns, support layers, and
  unresolved field material

## What it does not claim

- final source separation
- final instrument ownership
- complete reconstruction of organ, cello, violin, or piano stems

This example is meant to document the current research direction honestly and
compactly.
